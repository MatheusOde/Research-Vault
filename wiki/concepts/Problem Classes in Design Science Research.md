# Problem Classes in Design Science Research

## Summary

- A problem class is the abstraction that lets a DSR artifact contribute beyond one local case.
- Lacerda et al. define problem class as an organization of practical or theoretical problems containing useful artifacts for organizational action, whether evaluated or not.
- Problem classes support generalization because real problems are contextual, but can share structural features with other problems.
- In DSR, artifact knowledge becomes more reusable when tied to a class of problems, its limits, and its conditions of use.

## How To Build

- Start with an initial practical or theoretical problem.
- Conduct problem awareness: repercussions, boundaries, interested actors, satisfactory-solution criteria.
- Review literature and known empirical solutions.
- Identify existing artifacts and their limitations.
- Organize the problem and related artifacts into a class that can guide artifact selection, construction, and evaluation.

## Generalization Logic

- A situated artifact is not automatically generalizable.
- Generalization requires abstracting reusable mechanisms, conditions, and heuristics from the specific case.
- Cross-context evaluation, simulation, or comparative cases can strengthen generalization.
- Claims should state where the artifact is expected to work and where evidence is still weak.
