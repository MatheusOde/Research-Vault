# PRISMA Reporting

## Summary
- PRISMA 2020 is a reporting guideline for systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
- It improves completeness and transparency of reporting but is not itself a conduct guide or methodological-quality tool.
- It centers on explaining why the review was done, what authors did, and what they found.

## Details
PRISMA 2020 provides a 27-item checklist, abstract checklist, expanded checklist, and flow diagram templates. Major reporting domains include title, abstract, rationale, objectives, eligibility criteria, information sources, search strategy, selection process, data collection, data items, risk of bias, effect measures, synthesis methods, reporting bias, certainty, study selection, characteristics, results, discussion, registration/protocol, support, competing interests, and data/code/material availability.

The expanded checklist gives more detailed elements, including reporting all search sources and dates, full search strategies, reviewer independence, automation tools, decision rules, synthesis grouping, heterogeneity exploration, sensitivity analyses, and material availability.
