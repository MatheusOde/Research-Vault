# Systematic Literature Review Protocol

## Summary
- A systematic literature review protocol is an a priori plan for the review process.
- Strong protocols improve transparency, reproducibility, team coordination, and bias control.
- Reusable elements include goals/research questions, phenomena and synonyms, search strategy, screening criteria, data extraction, coding rules, quality appraisal, synthesis plan, and reporting plan.

## Details
Bandara and Syed frame the protocol as a living, end-to-end blueprint, not just a coding guide. It should include review goals, key topic areas, search sources/fields/strings, screening heuristics, extraction summary, tool setup, coding cycles, coding rules, codebooks, coder corroboration or intercoder reliability, and reporting plans.

Thomé et al. treat the protocol as part of planning and problem formulation in operations management SLRs. Their protocol includes research topic/questions, search strategy, inclusion/exclusion criteria, retrieval methods, coding, statistical procedures, and qualitative treatment.

Torres-Carrión et al. adapt Kitchenham/Bacca-style SLR procedure and add a conceptual pre-analysis that feeds the protocol: research questions, inclusion/exclusion criteria, data extraction form, and journal selection.
