# Enterprise Modeling for ERP Implementation

## Summary
- Enterprise modelling externalizes enterprise structures, behavior, knowledge, processes, goals, actors, resources, rules, concepts, and technical components.
- For ERP work, enterprise modelling is reusable because ERP projects need to align packaged system capabilities with local goals, processes, roles, rules, and data.
- EKD/4EM-style models offer a multi-view way to avoid treating ERP as only software configuration.
- Decision knowledge processing extends this idea into architectural decision capture, dependency graphs, rationale, and reuse for IT service proposals and projects.

## Details
Sandkuhl presents enterprise modelling as computerized models for solving a problem at hand: understanding current state, identifying improvements, preparing process innovation, and beginning information-system development. The 4EM framework includes goal, process, actors/resources, concepts, business rules, and technical components models, with participatory modelling as a core practice.

Carvalho and Guerrini apply EKD to ERP implementation networks. Their model uses the same basic multi-view logic to structure ERP goals, rules, processes, actors/resources, concepts, and technical requirements.

Zimmermann et al. focus not on ERP directly but on architectural knowledge management. Their reusable contribution is a decision point graph/metamodel for capturing architecture and outsourcing design decisions, dependencies, rationale, uncertainty, and review.
