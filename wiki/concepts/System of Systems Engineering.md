# System of Systems Engineering

## Summary
- System of Systems Engineering (SoSE) adapts systems engineering to collections of independent constituent systems that jointly deliver capabilities unavailable to individual systems alone.
- Recurrent SoS characteristics include operational independence, managerial independence, distribution, evolutionary development, emergence, interdependence, and interoperability.
- SoSE problems are hard because no single authority may control all constituent systems, requirements and interfaces evolve, and SoS-level behavior may emerge from interactions.
- Model-based SoSE needs techniques for heterogeneous models, partial information, evolving architectures, simulation, verification, and SoS-level test objectives.

## Details
Nielsen et al. frame SoSE as a subfield of systems engineering focused on boundaries and interactions between independent, distributed, evolving constituent systems and stakeholders. The field must account for both technical issues, such as interfaces and integration, and management issues, such as governance, ownership, and stakeholder conflict.

Useful dimensions for analyzing SoS include autonomy, independence, distribution, evolution, dynamic reconfiguration, emergence, interdependence, and interoperability. These dimensions help decide what kinds of modeling, simulation, testing, and verification are needed.

Torkjazi and Raz build on SoSE to define [[wiki/concepts/System of Autonomous Systems|System of Autonomous Systems]], where AI/ML-enabled autonomy compounds SoS challenges.
