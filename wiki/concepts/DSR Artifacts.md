# DSR Artifacts

## Summary

- In [[wiki/concepts/Design Science Research|Design Science Research]], artifacts are designed objects that embed a solution to a relevant problem.
- Classic artifact types are constructs, models, methods, and instantiations.
- `DSR disciplina.pdf` also treats design proposition as an artifact-like theoretical output.
- Artifacts are evaluated by utility, quality, efficacy, fit with environment, and contribution to a problem class.

## Types

- Construct: vocabulary/concepts for describing domain problems and solutions.
- Model: propositions or representations relating constructs; may represent a problem or solution.
- Method: structured steps, algorithms, or heuristics for performing a task.
- Instantiation: concrete realization of an artifact in a real or simulated environment.
- Design proposition: structured claim linking problem, context, artifact features, mechanism, and intended outcome; status as core artifact type is less settled in these sources.

## Artifact Logic

- Artifact has objectives, internal environment, and external environment.
- Internal environment contains components, rules, and organization of the artifact.
- External environment is where the artifact operates and must satisfy constraints.
- Useful artifacts should be abstracted enough to inform a [[wiki/concepts/Problem Classes in Design Science Research|problem class]], but grounded enough to solve a situated problem.
