# Systematic Review Search Reproducibility

## Summary
- Search reproducibility means another researcher can understand and rerun the review search closely enough to verify or update it.
- Required elements include exact sources, platforms, search fields, search dates, full search strings, filters/limits, and selection flow.
- Engineering SLRs often fail here by listing platforms instead of databases, omitting full search strings, omitting dates, or searching too few sources.

## Details
PRISMA 2020 requires specifying all databases, registers, websites, organizations, reference lists, and other sources searched or consulted, plus dates. It also requires full search strategies for all databases/registers/websites, including filters and limits.

Phillips et al. show that engineering SLR reporting often includes source lists and general search concepts, but frequently omits full database search strings, search dates, risk-of-bias assessment, multiple reviewers, and protocol registration. They also stress that reporting a platform such as Engineering Village or Web of Science is not enough unless the specific databases and platform configuration are clear.

The engineering education sub-analysis finds similar weaknesses: many SLRs search only one database/repository/platform, report publisher platforms rather than disciplinary databases, or give general themes rather than reproducible search strings.
