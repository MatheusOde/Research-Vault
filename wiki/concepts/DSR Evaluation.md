# DSR Evaluation

## Summary

- DSR evaluation checks whether an artifact works for its intended problem and environment.
- Evaluation is not optional: a DSR artifact needs evidence of utility, quality, efficacy, or pragmatic validity.
- Evaluation should be aligned with artifact type, performance demands, and context of use.
- Scientific rigor in the research process does not guarantee pragmatic validity of the artifact in organizations.

## Evaluation Forms

- Observational: case study or field study of artifact use.
- Analytical: static analysis, architecture analysis, optimization, dynamic analysis.
- Experimental: controlled experiment or simulation.
- Test: functional/black-box or structural/white-box tests.
- Descriptive: informed argument or scenarios.
- Focus group: exploratory refinement or confirmatory utility assessment.

## Validity Requirements

- State artifact objectives clearly.
- Make internal environment explicit.
- Make external environment and operating conditions explicit.
- Define metrics or criteria for satisfactory solution before evaluation where possible.
- Explain how artifact can be tested.
- Describe mechanisms that produce and measure outcomes.
- Document failures, limitations, adjustments, and bias risks.
