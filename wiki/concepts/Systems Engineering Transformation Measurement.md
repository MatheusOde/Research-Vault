# Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement

## Summary
- Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement tracks whether an organization is progressing toward effective Systems Engineering adoption.
- Current Systems Engineering transformation literature has weak measurement focus, so generic organizational transformation criteria are being adapted.
- Measurement should cover both transformation process progress and operational engineering impact.
- Candidate criteria require tailoring to concrete engineering contexts, artifacts, datapoints, and KPIs.

## Details
Graessler and Grewe identify 69 criteria from organizational transformation literature that are applicable to Systems Engineering transformation. These include organization-internal, PMTS/process-methods-tools-standards, portfolio, governance, culture, and cross-sectional categories.

The concept is not just maturity scoring. It includes program monitoring, change management, leadership, collaboration, roles, process optimization, measurement documentation, supplier/customer interfaces, and engineering performance outcomes.

Important distinction: process-oriented transformation measurement asks whether transformation activities are happening; product/operational impact measurement asks whether engineering work improved, such as better quality, faster engineering performance, or higher innovation level.
