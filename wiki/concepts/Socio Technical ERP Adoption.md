# Socio Technical ERP Adoption

## Summary
- ERP and PPC systems are socio-technical systems: outcomes depend on system capabilities, human acceptance, organizational context, and interface fit.
- User acceptance factors often missing from implementation strategies include relative advantage, compatibility, appropriate complexity, result demonstrability, data locatability, user authorization, system self-efficacy, and social influence.
- ERP go-live can temporarily increase absenteeism and turnover when training, tests, leadership support, and change management are weak.
- SMEs may recognize ERP/BI benefits yet underuse decision-support capabilities, especially when knowledge accumulation and training are weak.

## Details
Schuh et al. synthesize user acceptance models into 18 socio-technical requirements and find current IS implementation strategies cover only part of them. This warns against implementation checklists that overemphasize technical quality while neglecting actual usage conditions.

Simioni provides a mixed-method SAP case where absenteeism and turnover rise during early go-live, then stabilize as support, training, and leadership improve. This gives concrete human-outcome metrics for ERP change stress.

Antoniadis et al. show Greek SMEs perceive ERP advantages in data integration, reliable information, and time saving, but business intelligence capabilities are not fully exploited and years of ERP use do not correlate with perceived BI advantage.
