# Design Science Research

## Summary

- Design Science Research (DSR) is a research approach for designing, developing, evaluating, and communicating artifacts that solve relevant problems.
- DSR builds prescriptive knowledge: how things should be designed to achieve goals, rather than only how things are or why they behave as they do.
- Core tension: DSR must balance rigor and relevance by producing usable artifacts with explicit scientific contribution.
- Reusable knowledge comes from relating an artifact to a [[wiki/concepts/Problem Classes in Design Science Research|problem class]], not only a single local problem.

## Process Variants

- Peffers et al.: problem identification and motivation, define objectives, design and development, demonstration, evaluation, communication.
- Lacerda et al.: conscientizacao, sugestao, desenvolvimento, avaliacao, conclusao, with communication from Peffers et al. integrated.
- Teaching protocol in `DSR disciplina.pdf`: expands to 12 steps including systematic literature review, artifact/problem-class configuration, learning explication, and generalization.

## Distinctions

- Compared with traditional case study, DSR aims to prescribe and design rather than only explore, describe, or explain.
- Compared with traditional action research, DSR does not always require collaboration or implementation, but does require explicit artifact evaluation.
- Case study and action research can still be used inside DSR when subordinated to artifact construction, evaluation, or formalization.
