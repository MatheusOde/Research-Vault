# Research Draft

## Summary

- Current draft has a clear DSR shape: problem class, artifact, instantiation, proof of concept, evaluation, and learning.
- Strongest thesis spine: sociotechnical alignment in systems integration, operationalized through a 4EM-based prescriptive method.
- ERP should remain a reference context, not the full boundary of contribution.
- Web prototype should be scoped as an evaluable instantiation of the method, not as a product.
- Main next risk is evaluation feasibility: proof-of-concept scenario and panel access must be specified early.

## Draft Positioning

The draft narrows the prior broad research map into a concrete proposal:

- Domain: systems integration in organizations.
- Empirical context: ERP-related integration.
- Problem: sociotechnical misalignment between executive and IT perspectives.
- Artifact: prescriptive 4EM-based method.
- Instantiation: web prototype.
- Evaluation: ERP proof of concept plus academic expert panel.

This is more specific than the earlier [[wiki/syntheses/Mestrado Research Map|Mestrado Research Map]], which still treated the central phenomenon and artifact type as open.

## Method Architecture

The method should preserve traceability across this chain:

problem situation -> problem class -> literature evidence -> artifact requirements -> 4EM method steps -> web prototype screens/records -> proof-of-concept evidence -> expert judgments -> refined method

The key design constraint is keeping the method independent from the prototype. The prototype exists to make the method executable and observable.

## Next Work Items

- Define proof-of-concept ERP scenario with actors, processes, rules, data/concepts, systems, and central misalignment.
- Convert the draft method into a versioned artifact spec: method v0.1.
- Build a requirements matrix linking literature support, 4EM dimension, method capability, prototype feature, and evaluation evidence.
- Draft expert-panel instrument around clarity, coherence, utility, applicability, completeness, traceability, decision support, transferability, and prototype adequacy.
