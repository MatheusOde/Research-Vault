# PRISMA 2020 Expanded Checklist

## Summary
- Expanded reporting checklist for PRISMA 2020, derived from Page et al.'s PRISMA 2020 statement.
- Covers 27 reporting items across title, abstract, introduction, methods, results, discussion, and other information.
- Emphasizes transparent reporting of eligibility criteria, information sources, full search strategies, selection process, data collection, risk of bias, synthesis methods, certainty assessment, protocol/registration, support, competing interests, and data/code/material availability.
- Distinguishes essential reporting elements from additional elements that improve completeness and usability.

## Details
This source is a reporting checklist, not a conduct manual. It says systematic reviews should identify themselves in the title, state explicit objectives or questions, report eligibility criteria, name all information sources and search dates, and present complete search strategies for all databases/registers/websites.

The methods section expands PRISMA 2020 by requiring detail about reviewer independence, disagreement resolution, automation tools, data extraction rules, risk-of-bias tools, effect measures, synthesis decisions, heterogeneity exploration, sensitivity analyses, reporting-bias assessment, and certainty assessment.

The results section requires a study flow account, preferably a flow diagram; excluded-study rationale for studies that appear eligible; characteristics and risk-of-bias assessments for included studies; and synthesis results, including heterogeneity and sensitivity analyses where applicable.

The checklist also requires protocol/registration information, protocol amendments, support/funder role, competing interests, and availability of data, code, and materials.
