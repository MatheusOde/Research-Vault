# Ram et al. 2013 - ERP CSFs and Performance

## Summary
- Argues ERP implementation success and post-implementation organizational performance are separate dependent variables.
- Tests whether four commonly cited CSFs are truly critical: project management, training/education, business process reengineering, and system integration.
- Uses structural equation modelling on survey data from 217 organizations.
- Finds some CSFs may not drive implementation success directly but can still matter for performance improvement; implementation success can mediate CSF effects on performance.

## Details
The key contribution is conceptual discipline: a factor should not be called critical unless satisfactory attention to it leads to implementation success or performance outcomes. Project delivery measures include time, budget, expectations, and user satisfaction. Performance outcomes include operational, financial, customer-service, and long-term advantages.

The paper is a strong anchor for distinguishing go-live/project success from actual realized business value.
