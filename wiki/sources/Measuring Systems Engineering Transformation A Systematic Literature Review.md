# Measuring Systems Engineering Transformation: A Systematic Literature Review

## Summary
- Graessler and Grewe review measurement approaches for organizational transformation and assess transferability to [[wiki/concepts/Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement|Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement]].
- The paper argues that established Systems Engineering transformation approaches do not yet focus enough on measurement and monitoring.
- From 87 consolidated transformation criteria, 69 are judged applicable to Systems Engineering transformation.
- Criteria are clustered into dimensions and categories spanning organization, PMTS/process-methods-tools-standards, portfolio, governance, culture, and cross-sectional factors.
- Expert interviews confirm broad applicability but stress that criteria must be specified for the actual engineering context, especially CPPS engineering practice.

## Details
The paper uses PRISMA to search for transformation measurement literature. It starts with Systems Engineering-specific searches but broadens to organizational and enterprise transformation because the Systems Engineering measurement literature is sparse.

The review identifies categories including collaboration, governance structure, leadership commitment, vision, mindset and roles, coaching and mentoring, self-organized teams, external environment, process optimization, measurement/monitoring, and documentation. The paper also distinguishes output-oriented performance and finance factors from process-oriented transformation monitoring.

The authors emphasize a gap between generic change-management measurement and Systems Engineering transformation. Many criteria from business transformation are qualitative, often measured by guided interviews and Likert-scale questionnaires. For industrial use, they need operationalization into observable artifacts, datapoints, and Systems Engineering KPIs.

The source is most useful for building a transformation measurement framework, not for prescribing a finished one. It supports the claim that [[wiki/concepts/Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement|Systems Engineering Transformation Measurement]] needs both process progress metrics and operational engineering impact metrics.

## Research Gaps
- Further work needed to derive criteria from industrial Systems Engineering practice rather than only adapting generic transformation literature.
- Qualitative criteria need quantification or clearer assessment bases.
- Operational engineering impact needs more attention, including engineering performance, quality, and innovation outcomes.
- Existing Systems Engineering maturity models need integration with the identified criteria into an overarching transformation performance model.
