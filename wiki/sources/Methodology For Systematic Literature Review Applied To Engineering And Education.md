# Methodology For Systematic Literature Review Applied To Engineering And Education

## Summary
- Proposes an SLR methodology for engineering and education, adapted from Kitchenham and Bacca.
- Adds a preliminary conceptual analysis using the `mentefacto conceptual` to derive search words, synonyms, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and database scripts.
- Organizes SLR into planning, conducting, and reporting phases.
- Includes a case study on gestural interaction for children in inclusive educational settings.

## Details
The paper argues that novice researchers need support moving from their personal current state of knowledge to a field-level current state of the problem. It proposes using research questions and a `mentefacto conceptual` before the main SLR to structure the search space.

Planning includes identifying review need, current problem state, research questions, conceptual map, related SLRs, review protocol, inclusion/exclusion criteria, data extraction form, and journal selection. Conducting includes research identification, primary-study selection, quality assessment, data extraction/monitoring, and data synthesis/monitoring. Reporting follows Kitchenham-style components such as title, abstract, background, questions, methods, included/excluded studies, results, discussion, conclusions, and appendices.

The `mentefacto conceptual` defines a concept through properties, upper class, excluded neighboring classes, and subclasses. The paper uses it to produce search terms and synonyms, then convert them into semantic search structures and database-specific scripts for sources such as Web of Science, Scopus, and Google Scholar.
