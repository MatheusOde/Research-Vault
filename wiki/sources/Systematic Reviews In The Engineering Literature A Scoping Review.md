# Systematic Reviews In The Engineering Literature: A Scoping Review

## Summary
- Scoping review of systematic reviews across engineering literature.
- Searches Compendex, Inspec, and ERIC; final dataset contains 3,066 articles.
- Finds SRs are rapidly growing in engineering, especially since 2015.
- Finds reporting/method quality is often low, making many engineering SRs irreproducible.

## Details
The review asks how much SR methods are used in engineering and how closely engineering SRs follow established guidelines. It includes journal articles and conference papers in English that identify as SRs or meta-analyses and have an engineering focus, excluding software engineering and computer science.

Results show 93% of included SRs were published from 2015 onward. Industrial, civil, biomedical, environmental, and engineering education were the highest-count disciplines. The most frequently searched sources were Scopus, Web of Science, and ScienceDirect.

The authors find strong reporting for source lists and general search concepts, but weak reporting for registered protocols, librarian/database expert involvement, risk-of-bias assessment, multiple reviewers/interrater reliability, full search strings, and search dates. They note that listing platforms rather than specific databases undermines search reproducibility.

The authors recommend that engineering authors learn review typologies, use reporting/conduct guidelines such as PRISMA and Kitchenham-style guidance, collaborate with librarians/search experts, and that publishers require clearer SR reporting and method review.
