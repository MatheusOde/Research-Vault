# How To Conduct A Systematic Literature Review: A Quick Guide For Computer Science Research

## Summary
- Practical guide for early-stage computer science and software engineering researchers conducting systematic literature reviews.
- Divides SLR work into planning and conducting phases.
- Planning includes protocol definition, PICOC keywords/synonyms, research questions, digital library selection, inclusion/exclusion criteria, quality assessment, and data extraction form design.
- Conducting includes search string construction, study gathering, duplicate removal, selection/refinement, quality assessment, data extraction, analysis, and reporting.

## Details
The paper treats the protocol as the first SLR step because it defines procedures, logs planned activities, supports peer review, and improves replicability. PICOC is presented as a way to translate research objectives into searchable keywords and research questions.

For computer science, it lists common database sources including Scopus, Web of Science, EI Compendex, IEEE Xplore, and ACM Digital Library. It recommends defining inclusion and exclusion criteria before reviewing to reduce bias, while allowing cautious adjustment if necessary.

Quality assessment is described as a checklist with numerical scales. Data extraction should align with research questions and can capture research type, process phases, technology/framework/platform, application domain, gaps, challenges, findings, and evaluation method.

The paper recommends building search strings by OR-ing synonyms within PICOC elements and AND-ing conceptual groups. It highlights bibliographic exports, reference managers, duplicate removal, title/abstract screening, full-text quality assessment, and tools such as Parsif.al and VOSviewer.
