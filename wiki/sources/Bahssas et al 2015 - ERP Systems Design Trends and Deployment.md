# Bahssas et al. 2015 - ERP Systems Design Trends and Deployment

## Summary
- Reviews ERP evolution from inventory control, MRP, MRP II, client/server ERP, ERP II, SOA, web ERP, cloud ERP, and mobile ERP.
- Compares traditional ERP architectures: two-tier, three-tier, web-based, six-tier, cloud, and mobile.
- Identifies technology trends affecting ERP: web technologies, programming languages, mobile, cloud, security, and in-memory computing.
- Claims six-tier, cloud, and mobile ERP improve flexibility, remote access, cost, and legacy integration, but security remains a major issue.

## Details
The paper is a qualitative review of ERP designs and vendor trends. Its strongest reuse is as a compact architecture timeline: two-tier improves cost and direct communication but scales poorly; three-tier improves scalability and reuse; web-based ERP supports browser access and integration; six-tier adds decoupling via DAL/BLL/DSL/DML/component/application layers; cloud ERP shifts infrastructure to IaaS/PaaS/SaaS; mobile ERP adds device-specific presentation and XML-based exchange.
