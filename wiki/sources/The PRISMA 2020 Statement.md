# The PRISMA 2020 Statement

## Summary
- Updates PRISMA 2009 with guidance reflecting new methods for identifying, selecting, appraising, and synthesizing studies.
- Provides a 27-item checklist, abstract checklist, expanded checklist, and revised flow diagrams.
- Applies mainly to systematic review reporting, not review conduct or methodological-quality assessment.
- New or expanded areas include automation tools, full search strategies for all sources, synthesis methods, certainty assessment, competing interests, and data/code/material availability.

## Details
The statement defines systematic reviews as reviews using explicit, systematic methods to collate and synthesize findings addressing a clearly formulated question. It says systematic reviews should provide transparent, complete, and accurate accounts of why the review was done, what was done, and what was found.

PRISMA 2020 is designed primarily for systematic reviews of health intervention studies, but many items are applicable to other intervention reviews and objectives such as etiology, prevalence, or prognosis. It is relevant to reviews with or without statistical synthesis and to mixed-methods reviews, though qualitative synthesis guidelines may also be needed.

The statement explicitly says PRISMA 2020 should not be used as a conduct guide or as a tool to assess methodological quality. It recommends using it early in writing to ensure required information is captured.

Notable changes from 2009 include moving protocol/registration to Other Information, requiring full search strategies for all databases/registers/websites, reporting reviewer numbers and independence, automation tools, outcome/result selection methods, detailed synthesis methods, excluded-study rationale, certainty assessment, competing interests, and data/code/material availability.
