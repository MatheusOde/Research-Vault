# A Scoping Review Of Engineering Education Systematic Reviews

## Summary
- Sub-analysis of a broader engineering SLR scoping review, focused on engineering education.
- Identifies 276 engineering education SLRs/meta-analyses.
- Finds 93% were published during 2015-2022.
- Finds poor adherence to established reporting practices, especially protocol registration, search reproducibility, risk-of-bias assessment, and librarian/search-expert involvement.

## Details
The paper frames SLRs as formalized literature reviews that require predefined protocols, relevant document identification, selection and analysis, data extraction, and synthesis to answer specific research questions.

The review uses a protocol registered on OSF and searches Compendex, Inspec, and ERIC. It extracts descriptive and quality-related items, including whether studies state research questions, register protocols, list inclusion/exclusion criteria, report search dates, provide full search strings, list databases/platforms, use PRISMA flow diagrams, assess risk of bias, and report multiple reviewers/interrater reliability.

The authors find that engineering education SLRs are globally distributed and growing, but many do not report enough detail for reproducibility. Common problems include searching only one database or platform, reporting platforms rather than specific databases, weak or incomplete search strings, no protocol registration, and little explicit librarian/database expert involvement.

Recommendations include continuing education on review types and reporting standards, greater librarian/search-expert involvement, and publisher advocacy to require reporting documents and recruit search-method experts as reviewers.
