# Klaus et al. 2000 - What is ERP

## Summary
- The paper argues that ERP lacked a stable agreed definition in IS scholarship around 2000.
- It proposes understanding ERP through multiple views: software commodity, enterprise integration objective, and infrastructure for business solutions.
- ERP software is characterized by configurable packaged application software, integrated database, broad functionality, process orientation, multi-industry/multi-country support, and recurring transaction support.
- The paper traces ERP from MRP and MRP II through CIM and broader enterprise integration.

## Details
The authors combine historical analysis, meta-analysis of IS literature, and an expert survey to clarify ERP as an academic and practical term. They emphasize that ERP is not only a manufacturing-planning descendant but also a broader integration infrastructure.

Their source review shows early ERP research focused on implementation, teaching, case studies, and emerging topics such as knowledge management, SMEs, SCM, maintenance, and e-business.
