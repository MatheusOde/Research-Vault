# Panetto et al. 2016 - Future Interoperable Enterprise Systems

## Summary
- Position paper on next-generation enterprise information systems under rising socio-economic and technological complexity.
- Defines NG EIS as federated, omnipresent, model-driven, open, reconfigurable, aware, and inherently interoperable.
- Extends EIS generations from application-centric, data-centric, process-centric, human-centric, things-centric, to everything-centric systems.
- Identifies five main research challenges: context awareness, semantic interoperability, cyber-physical systems, cloud-based systems, and interoperability assessment.
- Notes semantic interoperability remains difficult in practice; automatic reconciliation of semantic heterogeneity is still weak and often manual.

## Details
The article frames future enterprise systems as interplay networks of people, data, things, and services. It argues interoperability is not an add-on but a necessary capability for ad-hoc collaboration, model federation, integration of data/information/knowledge resources, and interaction among smart objects, agents, and avatars.
