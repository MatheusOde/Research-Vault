# Simioni 2024 - SAP Absenteeism and Turnover Post Go Live

## Summary
- Mixed-method study of SAP implementation in a Brazilian fuel retail group, focusing on absenteeism and turnover after go-live.
- Quantitative data show initial increases in absenteeism and turnover during early go-live, followed by stabilization after support/training improvements.
- Qualitative interviews with 23 current/former employees identify cultural resistance, formalized processes, insufficient practical training, data migration issues, overload, and insufficient testing.
- Later go-live phases improved due to lessons learned, leadership support, workshops, and more structured training.
- Most of the 8 former employees interviewed said the SAP change directly influenced their decision to leave.

## Details
This is a useful concrete case for human impacts of ERP implementation. Company 1 had the largest early disruption; Company 2 benefited from prior lessons; Company 3 was the most stable. Interview themes emphasize that formalization and governance can feel like bureaucracy unless accompanied by leadership support, scenario testing, and practical training.
