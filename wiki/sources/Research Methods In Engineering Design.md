# Research Methods In Engineering Design

## Summary
- Systematic literature review of recent engineering design papers to map research methods, aims, data collection, and instruments.
- Analyzes 68 papers from four design/engineering design journals across a one-year window.
- Finds high diversity of aims and approaches, with no single methodological standard.
- Qualitative methods are common, but quantitative, mixed, and analytical approaches also appear.

## Details
The paper asks what the current landscape of research methods in engineering design is. It examines research goals, experimental approaches, data-collection methods, and data-collection instruments.

Research goals include studying/proposing methodology, studying a design aspect, developing/evaluating products, proposing recommendations/guidelines, proposing theory, and proposing frameworks or taxonomies. Common goals were methodology work and delving into specific aspects of design.

Research approaches include analytical, quantitative, mixed, and qualitative. The sample includes case studies, experiments, ethnography, phenomenology, hermeneutics, grounded theory, and action research. Case studies are pervasive, especially in qualitative and analytical work.

Data collection methods include observation, simultaneous verbalization, technical documents, physical objects, questionnaires, and interviews. Instruments include measurements, audio/video/photos, eye tracking, software/simulation, human input from users/participants, expert/designer opinions, and workshops.

The authors conclude that engineering design has no accepted standard method and that doctoral training should include qualitative and quantitative methods because engineering design is socio-technical and context-dependent.
