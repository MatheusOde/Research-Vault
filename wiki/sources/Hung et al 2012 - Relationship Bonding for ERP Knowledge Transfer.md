# Hung et al. 2012 - Relationship Bonding for ERP Knowledge Transfer

## Summary
- Examines ERP implementation as a knowledge-transfer problem between implementing firm and consultants.
- Proposes that relationship bonding factors shape knowledge transfer climate, which affects knowledge transfer outcomes.
- Surveyed 174 respondents and used PLS analysis.
- Supported factors: top management support, internal incentives, consultant industry experience, consultant project management capability, and knowledge transfer climate effect on knowledge transfer.
- Not supported: interdepartmental coordination and consultant reward system effects on knowledge transfer climate.

## Details
The study argues best practices and consultants do not guarantee ERP success because ERP requires knowledge exchange: consultants transfer ERP knowledge while the client transfers local process/business logic. A positive climate makes participants willing to share and receive knowledge.
