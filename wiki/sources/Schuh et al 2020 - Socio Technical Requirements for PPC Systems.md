# Schuh et al. 2020 - Socio Technical Requirements for PPC Systems

## Summary
- Analyzes why users often fail to realize the potential of production planning and control information systems.
- Frames PPC/ERP/MES/APS as socio-technical systems involving system, human, and interface factors.
- Synthesizes IDT, TTF, UTAUT, and TAM into 18 socio-technical user acceptance requirements.
- Finds implementation strategies cover only 7 of 18 requirements completely and individual approaches cover no more than 33%.
- Concludes current implementation strategies are incomplete, hard to operationalize, and too ERP-focused relative to MES/APS/PPC systems.

## Details
The 18 requirements include anxiety, appropriate complexity, compatibility, data locatability, data quality, external control, intrinsic motivation, job-fit, output quality, production timeliness, relative advantage, result demonstrability, social influence, system self-efficacy, system reliability, trialability, user authorization, and usability.

The paper is especially useful for diagnosing disuse and misuse: disuse can arise from weak social influence, authorization, or visible relative advantage; misuse can arise from weak system self-efficacy or data locatability.
