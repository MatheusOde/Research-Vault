# Antoniadis et al. 2015 - ERP and BI Usage by SMEs

## Summary
- Studies ERP adoption and BI usage among 37 SMEs in Western Macedonia, Greece during economic crisis conditions.
- Finds SMEs recognize ERP benefits such as data integration, reliable information, time saving, and control.
- Finds ERP business intelligence capabilities are underutilized; 11 of 23 BI variables scored below the overall BI average.
- Years of ERP usage did not correlate with perceived ERP advantages or BI capabilities, suggesting weak economies of knowledge.
- Major adoption factors include provider support, software configuration, interdepartmental communication/cooperation, training, needs assessment, and resources.

## Details
The most important perceived ERP advantages were data/information integration, reliable information, and time saving. Cost reduction and operational-resource saving were rated lower, creating tension with claims that ERP primarily delivers cost savings.

Most valued BI capabilities were aggregated result tools, import/export with other systems, accounting/economic management tools, reporting, OLAP, and data extraction. Underused capabilities included property management, investment tools, simulation, risk assessment, fuzzy logic, and collaborative decision-making.
