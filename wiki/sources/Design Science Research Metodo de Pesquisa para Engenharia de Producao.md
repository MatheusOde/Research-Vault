# Design Science Research Metodo de Pesquisa para Engenharia de Producao

## Summary

- Lacerda, Dresch, Proenca, and Antunes Junior introduce [[wiki/concepts/Design Science Research|Design Science Research]] as a rigorous method for Brazilian production engineering and operations management.
- The article frames Design Science as a science of the artificial: research should not only describe or explain systems, but design artifacts that achieve intended goals.
- It emphasizes [[wiki/concepts/Problem Classes in Design Science Research|problem classes]] as the unit that lets situated artifacts generate reusable knowledge.
- It distinguishes DSR from traditional case study and action research by objective, researcher role, empirical requirements, implementation requirements, and evaluation logic.
- It highlights [[wiki/concepts/DSR Evaluation|evaluation and validity]] as core: scientific rigor does not by itself guarantee pragmatic validity.

## Key Claims

- Production engineering needs methods that support prescriptive, artifact-building research, not only descriptive, explanatory, or predictive research.
- Design Science knowledge is pragmatic and prescriptive: it concerns how things should be to solve problems.
- A real organizational problem is singular, but problems and satisfactory solutions can share features that support generalization to a class of problems.
- DSR can use case study and action research as methods under a design-science agenda, but the agenda changes when the research aim is artifact construction/evaluation.
- DSR validity requires explicit internal environment, external environment, objectives, test procedure, and outcome-measure mechanisms.

## Concepts Extracted

- Design Science: epistemological base for developing knowledge about artifact conception and development.
- Design Science Research: method that operationalizes Design Science through artifact design, evaluation, and communication.
- Artifact types: constructs, models, methods, and instantiations.
- Problem class: organization of practical or theoretical problems that contain useful artifacts for organizational action, evaluated or not.
- Satisfactory solution: solution good enough for real contexts where optimal solutions are inaccessible or impractical.

## Method Extracted

- Conscientizacao: understand and formalize problem, boundaries, external environment, and satisfactory solution criteria.
- Sugestao: create candidate artifact alternatives and choose what to develop.
- Desenvolvimento: build the functional artifact and its internal environment.
- Avaliacao: rigorously verify artifact behavior against intended solution in the relevant environment.
- Conclusao: formalize lessons and communicate to academic and professional communities.
